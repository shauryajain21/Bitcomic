What is cryptocurrency? It’s money, stocks, and gold. Let’s go on each point

How is crypto money?

Around 350 BCE, Aristotle defined Sound Money as:

  1. Durable (physically stable, does not rot or rust, or melt )
  2. Transferrable (easy to transfer ownership from one person to another )
  3. Divisible (can be subdivided into smaller units of money)
  4. Must provide Intrinsic Value, i.e. “value independent and contained in the money itself.”
    1. Scarce (limited quantity available for use)
    2. Recognizable (easy to recognize and verify authenticity)
    3. Fungible(one unit of money can be substituted for another)

Bitcoin, dogecoin, and many of the cryptocurrencies perfectly satisfies each and every condition stated above.

Do you know about Pacioli Ledger? Pacioli Ledger's are everywhere in modern society. Let’s take this example to see a little better. Harry wants to send $100 to his friend jasmine. He’s doing the transaction through some clicks on his phone. In the backend, it’s just the $100 being written off from the Pacioli ledger of Harry and being written in Jasmine's Pacioli ledger. Through a series of entries in a Pacioli ledger, a hundred dollars is transferred from Henry to jasmine. In fact, our entire financial system in the modern world is based upon and is in fact only a system of Ledgers. Money is not something tangible money is in fact an entry on a ledger. Your money is an unsecured obligation. Its debt on a bank’s balance sheet. It's an entry on a ledger. The most secure ledger is *Drumroll* bitcoin’s. It is the blockchain, A digital public ledger maintaining all the transaction details encrypted (safe).

So yes, explaining the two paragraphs above: Bitcoin and other cryptos satisfy all metrics of ‘money’, the transaction of this money happens on extra secure blockchain not on some banks notepad.

But I have digital money on my phone and plastic money on my credit card. Okay it’s a very simple explanation: The money in your digital wallet is just the regular paper money converted to digital and bitcoin is completely digital money. We discussed that money is just some number written on a bank's ledger, Bitcoin is digital money written on a digital ledger controlled by No human being.

How is crypto gold?

It’s easy. Gold is not valuable because it’s shiny. (aluminiul is shiny). It’s valuable because it’s limited. There’s just a certain amount of gold that can be present at a certain time and that amount is very less.

Bitcoin is just the same, Something that is scarce and is digital and is more secure to keep. At any point of time there can be less than or equal 21 million bitcoins in this world. This scarcity makes it gold like.

How can crypto act like stocks?

Stocks represent stake in a company. An indigo stock represents 0.000000000027695% indigo as a company. A company releases its stock to sell that ownership. Everytime the company does something cool, the stock goes up.

This can be replicated in the crypto world as well. A new company can release it’s coin. This coin kind of represents a stake in the company. If the company does something awesome the coin goes up in price as the people like it’s work and the demand of that coin goes up but the amount of those coins remains the same so the value goes up. ETH is a stock of ethereum.

1. Crypto can be money as it fulfils all the propeties of money and is recorded on a decentralised public blockchain.
2. Bitcoin is scarce, just like gold, so it has value like gold.
3. Crypto can represent a stake in a company just like a stock so it can be that too.
