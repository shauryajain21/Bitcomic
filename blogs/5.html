Smart Contracts

What is a smart Contract

A smart contract is a self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement between buyer and seller being directly written into lines of code. The code and the agreements contained therein exist across a distributed, decentralized blockchain network. The code controls the execution, and transactions are trackable and irreversible.

Now let’s go around it more simply by this example…

The basic contract we use is to take loans from our banks. So when the bank gives us a loan, it takes our collateral, gives us a date to pay back by, and an interest rate. The bank then makes us sign a contract. This can be replicated in a blockchain through a smart contract. A smart contract can check the crypto balance in the wallet and ask to lock that balance, calculates interest for us, and asks us to pay back the amount by a date, and give us the crypto we need in the loan. As soon as we pay, it releases the collateral crypto and if we don’t it recovers the due amount by using the collateral crypto. This is completely controlled by few lines of code and not an institution like a bank.

Smart contract and traditional contract

A smart contract is different from a traditional contract performed via computers. Setting up an automatic money transfer order via online banking is not a smart contract arrangement, as either the customer or the bank may intervene, terminate or change the order at any time. Conversely, a smart contract is executed and performed autonomously without the parties being able to interfere or run the risk of it being tampered with. Accordingly, the autonomous nature of self-execution and performance rights and obligations are the fundamentals that make smart contracts "smart".

An example of the profound opportunity for banking the unbanked is the case of girls in Afghanistan who use their phones to get paid in bitcoin, without having to ask for male colleagues to accompany them to the bank for safety. Cryptocurrencies offer a reduced way to store and move value that is free of guardians that has never been possible before.

Smart contracts have certain practical advantages over traditional paper contracts:

Certainty:Since smart contracts are implemented by computer codes, there is no room for any ambiguous natural language may be used in traditional contracts.
SpeedLike any type of computer automation, smart contracts are executed and implemented almost instantly without human involvement.
CostAlthough there will be upfront costs for the development and implementation of the smart contract platform, the operating costs associated with the performance of smart contracts will be low, since there will be almost no human involvement at the performance phase.

Entire Defi systems and many other applications of crypto are based on smart contracts. Ethereum pioneered this technology and many other cryptos like Cardano and Tron are working on it. Through these smart contracts, we can build entire financial systems decentralized, and games with real crypto usage, have automatic payment systems in supply chains, and e-commerce systems decentralized.
